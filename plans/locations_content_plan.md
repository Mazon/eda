# World of Eda: Locations Content Plan

This plan outlines the detailed locations to be added to the World of Eda Gazetteer section.

---

## Overview

The World of Eda needs rich, detailed locations that GMs can use as adventure hubs, safe havens, and sources of conflict. Each location will include:

- Detailed descriptions with atmosphere and sensory details
- Multiple sub-locations (districts, shops, houses, etc.)
- Key NPCs with personalities, secrets, and quest hooks
- Ongoing events and conflicts
- GM-only secrets and plot hooks
- Random encounter tables specific to each location

---

## Location Structure Template

Each location will follow this structure:

```markdown

![Location Image](images/[image_name].png)

- Current tensions

|-----|-----------|
```

## Part I: Major City - Oakhaven

### Overview
Oakhaven is the largest settlement in the Fjordlands, built within the hollowed-out skull of a minor giant. It is a fragile alliance between three factions: the Order of the Veiled Breath, the Iron Covenant, and the Church of the Sun-Bringer.

### Districts & Sub-locations

#### 1. The Skull Gate (Main Entrance)
- Massive stone archway carved from the giant's jawbone
- Guarded by Iron Covenant soldiers wearing blindfolds
- Checkpoint where refugees are processed or turned away

#### 2. The Market District
- Central hub of commerce and daily life
- Muddy streets with permanent layer of ice
- **The Muddy Tankard**: Central tavern and inn
- **Orin's Smithy**: Weapons and armor (Iron Covenant approved)
- **Elara's Herbalist**: Potions, bandages, and questionable remedies
- **Garris' Goods**: General store with inflated prices
- **The Black Market**: Hidden beneath the market square (Grek's domain)

#### 3. The High Terrace
- Stone houses on the hill overlooking the city
- **Mayor's Manor**: Seat of local government, Mayor Valen's residence
- **The Archive**: Library of pre-plague knowledge (restricted access)
- **Temple of the Veiled Breath**: Headquarters of the Masked Ones
- **Sun-Bringer's Chapel**: Small, flickering flame maintained by dwindling faithful

#### 4. The Slums (The Mud-Pits)
- Tents and lean-tos outside the main walls
- **Soup Kitchen**: Run by Mara, feeds the desperate
- **Refugee Camp**: Overflowing with people fleeing the Long Winter
- Gang-controlled territories

#### 5. The Docks
- Frozen harbor with ships trapped in ice
- Ships now used as housing
- **Captain Jora's Smuggling Operation**: Ice fishing and contraband
- Fight clubs on the ice

#### 6. The Undercity (Sewers)
- Ancient drainage system beneath the city
- Home to giant rats, slime creatures, and worse
- **The Hollowed Nest**: Secret lair of infected refugees

### Key NPCs

#### Mayor Valen
- Appearance: Pale, sweating, wears velvet even in cold, uses a cane
- Personality: Cowardly but cunning, terrified of Hrothgar and his own people
- Secret: Infected by slow strain of Hollowed, hides black veins under collar
- Quest Hooks: Needs help covering up infection, wants players to investigate High Barrow

#### Captain Vorn (Head of Watch)
- Appearance: Obese, grease-stained tunic, missing an ear
- Personality: Bully who kisses up to power, extorts refugees
- Weakness: Deeply superstitious, magic tricks terrify him
- Quest Hooks: Wants players to eliminate rival gang, needs "protection" from supernatural threats

#### Mara (Priestess of the Hearth)
- Appearance: Young (20s), grey streak in hair from stress, robes stained with blood and mud
- Personality: Moral center of town, works 20 hours/day healing sick
- Quest Hooks: Fetch quests for medicine, eventually asks players to investigate sewers

#### Grek "The Rat" (Info Broker)
- Appearance: Tiny, hunchbacked, wears six layers of rags
- Location: The Muddy Tankard (darkest corner)
- Role: Knows everything entering/leaving city
- Price: Wants secrets or strange artifacts, not money
- Quest Hooks: Information trade, needs items from dangerous locations

#### Beren (Innkeeper)
- Appearance: Huge, one arm (lost to troll), bald head scarred by fire
- Personality: No-nonsense, protective of patrons
- Combat: Vaults bar with cudgel if fight breaks out
- Quest Hooks: Needs protection from extortion, knows old legends

#### Orin (Blacksmith)
- Appearance: Muscular, burn scars on arms, always wears apron
- Personality: Gruff but fair, secretly hates Iron Covenant
- Secret: Hiding a family heirloom that's actually a magical artifact
- Quest Hooks: Needs rare materials, wants players to deliver message to resistance

#### Elara (Herbalist)
- Appearance: Elderly woman with knowing eyes, fingers stained with herbs
- Personality: Cynical but helpful, knows more than she says
- Secret: Was once an Ashen Seer, left the order
- Quest Hooks: Needs ingredients from dangerous areas, knows about the plague

### Events & Conflicts

#### Current Tensions
1. Refugee Crisis: Hundreds arrive daily, food supplies dwindling
2. Faction Rivalry: Iron Covenant wants to purge "unclean" elements, Masked Ones resist
3. Hollowed Infection: Spreading through sewers, Mayor hiding the truth
4. Winter Worsening: Each day colder, fires harder to maintain

#### Ongoing Problems
- Food riots becoming more frequent
- Gangs controlling slums becoming more violent
- Iron Covenant conducting "purges" of suspected magic users
- Sun-Bringers' flame flickering dangerously low

#### Seasonal Events
- Midwinter Festival: Attempt to maintain morale, increasingly desperate
- The Thinning: Veil between worlds grows thin, supernatural activity increases
- Frost Moon: Wolves become more aggressive, attacks on city walls increase

### GM Secrets

1. **The Hollowed Queen**: A powerful entity in the sewers, controlling the infected. She was once a noblewoman who made a pact with the Old Night.

2. **The Sun-Shard**: Hidden beneath the Mayor's Manor. It's a fragment of the first sun that can temporarily reverse the Long Winter but attracts the Void.

3. **The Resistance**: Small group of citizens plotting to overthrow the Iron Covenant. Led by Orin's apprentice.

4. **The Giant's Memory**: The skull Oakhaven is built in still has fragments of consciousness. On rare nights, it whispers prophecies to those sleeping near the bone.

5. **The Plague's Origin**: The Dark Plague that wiped out 95% of humanity was released from beneath High Barrow. Something is stirring there again.

### Quest Hooks

1. **The Missing Children**: Children from the slums are disappearing. Investigation leads to the sewers and the Hollowed Queen.

2. **The Mayor's Secret**: Players discover Mayor Valen's infection. He offers them power/wealth to keep it secret.

3. **The Sun-Bringer's Last Hope**: The eternal flame is dying. Players must journey to find a way to reignite it.

4. **The Resistance's Call**: Orin's apprentice approaches players to join the fight against the Iron Covenant.

5. **The Giant's Whisper**: Players begin hearing the skull's whispers, leading them on a quest to uncover ancient knowledge.

## Part II: Village 1 - Frostspire

### Overview
A mountain village built around an ancient watchtower that predates the Age of Wonder. The villagers are descendants of the original guardians, maintaining a vigil against threats from the north.

### Sub-locations

#### 1. The Watchtower (Frostspire)
- Ancient stone tower reaching into the clouds
- Top floor houses the Ever-Watch lantern (magical light)
- Lower levels contain archives of pre-plague observations
- Secret basement with ancient technology

#### 2. The Village Square
- Central well with water that never freezes
- **The Frosty Mug**: Local tavern and gathering place
- **General Store**: Basic supplies, hunting equipment
- **The Watchman's Hall**: Meeting place for village council

#### 3. The Hunter's Quarter
- Homes of the village's hunters and scouts
- **The Trophy Hall**: Displays of legendary beasts killed
- **The Tannery**: Processing animal hides

#### 4. The Healer's Cottage
- Home of the village healer
- Garden of medicinal herbs (some only grow at this altitude)
- Secret basement with experimental remedies

#### 5. The Old Mine
- Abandoned iron mine, now home to something else
- Rumored to contain ancient weapons
- Entrance sealed by village elders

### Key NPCs

#### Elder Thorne (Village Leader)
- Appearance: Weathered face, white beard, missing two fingers
- Personality: Stern but fair, carries weight of generations
- Secret: Knows the tower's true purpose, has never told anyone
- Quest Hooks: Needs players to investigate strange lights in the north

#### Mira (Healer)
- Appearance: Young woman with gentle hands, always smells of herbs
- Personality: Compassionate but guarded about her methods
- Secret: Uses forbidden blood magic to heal the sick
- Quest Hooks: Needs rare ingredients, hiding a patient with mysterious illness

#### Kael (Head Hunter)
- Appearance: Scarred face, wears wolf pelt, carries massive bow
- Personality: Silent, efficient, respects strength
- Secret: Was once an Iron Covenant soldier, deserted
- Quest Hooks: Needs help tracking a beast, knows Iron Covenant movements

#### Jorin (Innkeeper)
- Appearance: Rotund, cheerful, missing teeth
- Personality: Gossip, knows everyone's business
- Secret: Spies for the Iron Covenant (reluctantly)
- Quest Hooks: Information broker, needs help with personal problem

#### Old Silas (Tower Keeper)
- Appearance: Ancient, blind, but moves with uncanny precision
- Personality: Speaks in riddles, seems to see more than blind should
- Secret: The tower speaks to him, shows him visions
- Quest Hooks: Cryptic warnings, needs players to fulfill prophecy

### Events & Conflicts

#### Current Tensions
1. The Ever-Watch lantern is flickering, causing panic
2. Hunters report strange creatures in the north
3. Iron Covenant demanding access to the tower
4. A stranger arrived claiming to be from Aethelgard

#### Ongoing Problems
- Food stores running low due to harsh winter
- Some villagers want to abandon the watchtower
- Mysterious illness affecting the children

#### Seasonal Events
- The Watch Festival: Annual celebration of the tower's vigil
- The Long Night: Winter solstice, when the veil thins most

### GM Secrets

1. **The Tower's Purpose**: Frostspire was built to contain a shard of the Old Night. The Ever-Watch lantern is the seal.

2. **The Stranger from Aethelgard**: Is actually a Glass-Singer, seeking the tower's knowledge to save their dying city.

3. **The Ancient Technology**: The tower contains a pre-plague device that could temporarily reverse the Long Winter, but using it would release the shard.

4. **The Children's Illness**: Caused by the weakening seal. The shard's influence is spreading.

5. **The Iron Covenant's True Goal**: They want to weaponize the shard, not contain it.

### Quest Hooks

1. **The Fading Light**: The Ever-Watch lantern is dying. Players must find a way to restore it before the seal breaks.

2. **The Stranger's Request**: The Glass-Singer needs help accessing the tower's archives.

3. **The Iron Covenant's Siege**: The Iron Covenant is preparing to attack. Players must help defend the village.

4. **The Beast in the North**: Hunters report a creature of impossible size. Players must hunt it.

5. **The Ancient Weapon**: Players discover the pre-plague device. They must decide whether to use it.

## Part III: Village 2 - Ashwater

### Overview
A fishing village built on the shores of a lake with water that turns to ash when disturbed. The villagers have adapted to this strange phenomenon and developed unique traditions around it.

### Sub-locations

#### 1. The Ash Lake
- Mysterious lake with grey, ash-like water
- Fish that live here have strange properties
- The water turns to ash when touched by the living
- Said to be connected to the Old Night

#### 2. The Village Proper
- Houses built on stilts to avoid the ash
- **The Ashen Net**: Local tavern and inn
- **The Drying Racks**: Where the strange fish are processed
- **The Shrine of Ashes**: Religious site where offerings are made

#### 3. The Fishermen's Docks
- Boats made of special treated wood
- **The Netmaker's Shop**: Creates nets that can handle ash-water
- **The Smokehouse**: Preserves the catch

#### 4. The Elder's House
- Largest building in the village
- Contains the village's history and traditions
- Secret room with forbidden knowledge

#### 5. The Ash Pits
- Where the ash from the lake is dumped
- Said to be bottomless
- Strange things sometimes crawl out

### Key NPCs

#### Elder Mara (Village Leader)
- Appearance: Ancient woman with ash-grey skin, sightless eyes
- Personality: Wise but terrifying, speaks in whispers
- Secret: Can see through the ash, knows what lies beneath
- Quest Hooks: Needs players to investigate something in the ash

#### Finn (Head Fisherman)
- Appearance: Strong, weathered, missing an ear to frostbite
- Personality: Practical, skeptical of outsiders
- Secret: Found something in the lake that he's keeping hidden
- Quest Hooks: Needs help with a catch, knows the lake's secrets

#### Lira (Netmaker)
- Appearance: Young woman with nimble fingers, always smells of tar
- Personality: Curious about the outside world
- Secret: Creating nets that can catch things other than fish
- Quest Hooks: Needs rare materials, wants to leave the village

#### Brother Silas (Shrine Keeper)
- Appearance: Thin, gaunt, wears robes of ash-grey cloth
- Personality: Fanatical, believes the ash is holy
- Secret: Performing rituals that are actually feeding the Old Night
- Quest Hooks: Needs players to retrieve "holy" items

#### The Stranger (Mysterious Visitor)
- Appearance: Wears a cloak that seems to absorb light
- Personality: Never speaks, communicates through gestures
- Secret: Is actually a Void-touched being
- Quest Hooks: Leads players to dangerous knowledge

### Events & Conflicts

#### Current Tensions
1. The ash is spreading further from the lake each year
2. Fish are becoming harder to catch
3. A stranger arrived and won't leave
4. The Iron Covenant has taken interest in the village

#### Ongoing Problems
- Food shortages as fish become scarce
- Some villagers want to abandon the lake
- Strange creatures emerging from the ash pits

#### Seasonal Events
- The Ash Festival: Celebration of the lake's bounty
- The Grey Tide: When the ash rises highest

### GM Secrets

1. **The Lake's Origin**: The lake is actually a tear in the fabric of reality, where the Old Night bleeds through.

2. **The Ash's Purpose**: The ash is the Old Night's attempt to consume the world. Each ash particle is a fragment of non-existence.

3. **The Stranger**: Is a Void-touched being trying to close the tear from the other side.

4. **The Fish**: Are actually fragments of the Old Night given form. Eating them grants visions but causes corruption.

5. **The Shrine's Rituals**: Brother Silas's rituals are actually widening the tear.

### Quest Hooks

1. **The Spreading Ash**: The ash is encroaching on the village. Players must find a way to stop it.

2. **The Stranger's Secret**: Players must discover who the stranger is and what they want.

3. **The Iron Covenant's Interest**: The Iron Covenant wants to weaponize the ash. Players must stop them.

4. **The Bottomless Pit**: Something is emerging from the ash pits. Players must investigate.

5. **The Ancient Ritual**: Elder Mara knows a ritual that could close the tear, but requires a terrible sacrifice.

## Part IV: Ancient Ruins - Aethelgard

### Overview
The legendary city of the Age of Wonder, now frozen in time beneath glass domes. Most believe it's a myth, but those who have seen it speak of impossible wonders and terrible dangers.

### Sub-locations

#### 1. The Glass Domes
- Massive domes of indestructible glass
- Inside, the city is perfectly preserved
- The air inside is breathable but strange
- Time flows differently inside

#### 2. The Central Plaza
- Heart of the ancient city
- **The Fountain of Memory**: Shows visions of the past
- **The Statue of the First**: Monument to the first enlightened
- **The Library of Echoes**: Contains all knowledge from the Age of Wonder

#### 3. The Residential District
- Preserved homes of the ancient inhabitants
- Some still contain personal belongings
- Ghostly echoes of the past can be seen and heard

#### 4. The Temple of Light
- Where the ancient gods were worshipped
- Contains artifacts of immense power
- Said to be where the Dark Plague originated

#### 5. The Undercity
- Beneath the main city
- Contains the machinery that maintained the domes
- Home to the Glass-Singers, survivors who stayed

### Key NPCs

#### The Glass-Singers
- Appearance: Translucent skin, eyes that reflect light
- Personality: Aloof, speak in songs
- Secret: They are slowly fading away
- Quest Hooks: Need help preserving their knowledge

#### The Echoes
- Appearance: Ghostly figures, barely visible
- Personality: Fragmented memories of the dead
- Secret: Can be communicated with through the right rituals
- Quest Hooks: Hold knowledge players need

#### The Guardian
- Appearance: Massive construct of glass and light
- Personality: Programmed to protect the city
- Secret: Is malfunctioning, becoming dangerous
- Quest Hooks: Players must repair or destroy it

### Events & Conflicts

#### Current Tensions
1. The domes are beginning to crack
2. The Glass-Singers are dying out
3. The Guardian is becoming unstable
4. The Iron Covenant is searching for the city

#### Ongoing Problems
- Time inside the domes is accelerating
- The echoes are becoming more aggressive
- Something is trying to get in from outside

#### Seasonal Events
- The Song of Glass: Annual ritual to maintain the domes
- The Memory Festival: When the echoes are strongest

### GM Secrets

1. **The City's Fate**: Aethelgard was preserved by a sacrifice of the entire population. Their souls power the domes.

2. **The Dark Plague**: Originated in the Temple of Light, released by an experiment gone wrong.

3. **The Glass-Singers**: Are the last descendants of the ancient inhabitants, bound to the city.

4. **The Guardian**: Contains the consciousness of the city's creator, slowly going mad.

5. **The Outside Threat**: The Old Night is trying to break in through the cracks in the domes.

### Quest Hooks

1. **The Cracking Domes**: Players must find a way to repair the domes before the city is destroyed.

2. **The Glass-Singers' Plea**: The Glass-Singers need players to help them preserve their knowledge.

3. **The Guardian's Madness**: Players must deal with the malfunctioning Guardian.

4. **The Temple's Secret**: Players must investigate the Temple of Light and uncover the plague's origin.

5. **The Iron Covenant's Search**: The Iron Covenant is closing in. Players must stop them from finding Aethelgard.

## Part V: Military Structures

### 1. Ravenwatch Tower

#### Overview
A border fortress guarding the southern approaches to the Fjordlands. It is the first line of defense against threats from the south.

#### Sub-locations

##### The Main Keep
- Central tower housing the commander
- Barracks for the garrison
- Armory and supply stores

##### The Watch Posts
- Three smaller towers surrounding the main keep
- Each manned by a squad of soldiers
- Connected by underground tunnels

##### The Dungeon
- Prison for captured enemies
- Said to be haunted by those who died there

#### Key NPCs

##### Commander Haldor
- Appearance: Scarred face, missing an eye, wears heavy armor
- Personality: Strict but fair, deeply loyal to the Iron Covenant
- Secret: Beginning to doubt the Covenant's methods
- Quest Hooks: Needs players to investigate strange occurrences

##### Lieutenant Elara
- Appearance: Young but experienced, sharp eyes
- Personality: Ambitious, wants to prove herself
- Secret: Reporting to the Inquisition
- Quest Hooks: Needs help with a secret mission

#### Events & Conflicts

##### Current Tensions
1. Raids from the south are increasing
2. Supplies are running low
3. Some soldiers are deserting
4. Strange lights seen at night

##### GM Secrets

1. **The Southern Threat**: The raids are being organized by a Void-touched warlord.

2. **The Deserters**: Are being taken by something in the woods.

3. **The Strange Lights**: Are actually a portal opening.

### 2. Ironhold Outpost

#### Overview
A small outpost established by the Iron Covenant to extend their influence. It is a symbol of their power and a source of fear for the locals.

#### Sub-locations

##### The Iron Fortress
- Small but heavily fortified building
- Houses a squad of Inquisitors
- Contains a prison and torture chamber

##### The Watch Tower
- Tall tower with a view of the surrounding area
- Used to spot "heretics" and magic users

#### Key NPCs

##### Inquisitor Vorn
- Appearance: Pale, gaunt, wears a blindfold
- Personality: Fanatical, believes he is doing the gods' work
- Secret: Has begun hearing voices
- Quest Hooks: Needs players to hunt a "heretic"

##### Sergeant Kael
- Appearance: Muscular, scarred, wears heavy armor
- Personality: Brutal but follows orders
- Secret: Hates the Inquisitors but fears them
- Quest Hooks: Can be turned against the Inquisition

#### Events & Conflicts

##### Current Tensions
1. The locals are planning a rebellion
2. The Inquisitors are becoming more aggressive
3. Supplies are running low
4. Something is hunting the soldiers at night

##### GM Secrets

1. **The Rebellion**: Is being organized by a former Inquisitor.

2. **The Voices**: Inquisitor Vorn is hearing are from the Old Night.

3. **The Hunter**: Is a Garou pack that has marked the outpost.

### 3. The Border Fort

#### Overview
An ancient fortification that marks the boundary between the Fjordlands and the Whispering Woods. It is neutral ground, maintained by a small group of rangers.

#### Sub-locations

##### The Main Gate
- Massive iron gates that have stood for centuries
- Covered in ancient runes
- Said to be impassable to the supernatural

##### The Ranger Station
- Home to the rangers who guard the border
- Contains maps and records of the woods
- Has a small shrine to the gods of the wild

##### The Watchtower
- Highest point in the area
- Provides a view of both the Fjordlands and the woods
- Said to be able to see into the ether

#### Key NPCs

##### Ranger Captain Thorne
- Appearance: Weathered face, grey hair, moves with grace
- Personality: Wise, knows the woods better than anyone
- Secret: Is actually centuries old, bound to the border
- Quest Hooks: Needs players to investigate something in the woods

##### Ranger Elara
- Appearance: Young, fierce, carries a bow
- Personality: Eager to prove herself
- Secret: Is secretly in love with a creature from the woods
- Quest Hooks: Needs help with a personal matter

#### Events & Conflicts

##### Current Tensions
1. Something is trying to cross the border
2. The Iron Covenant wants to take control of the fort
3. The rangers are divided on how to respond
4. The ancient runes are fading

##### GM Secrets

1. **The Border**: Is actually a seal against the Old Night.

2. **The Something**: Is a Void-touched being trying to enter the Fjordlands.

3. **The Captain**: Is the last of an ancient order of guardians.

## Part VI: Supernatural Landmarks

### 1. The Cursed Spring

#### Overview
A spring in the Whispering Woods with water that grants visions but drives those who drink it mad. It is said to be a tear of a dead god.

#### Description
- The water is clear but reflects things that aren't there
- The area around the spring is always cold
- Strange plants grow nearby, none of them edible
- Animals avoid the spring

#### Effects
- Drinking the water grants visions of the future or past
- Each drink causes permanent mental damage
- Eventually, the drinker becomes a hollow shell, a vessel for the dead god's whispers

#### GM Secrets

1. **The Spring's Origin**: Is the tear of a titan who died of grief.

2. **The Visions**: Are actually the titan's memories.

3. **The Madness**: Is the titan trying to possess the drinker.

#### Quest Hooks

1. **The Desperate Seeker**: Someone needs information only the spring can provide.
2. **The Mad Prophet**: A former drinker is spreading the titan's whispers.
3. **The Cure**: Players must find a way to purify the spring.

### 2. The Haunted Grove

#### Overview
A grove in the Whispering Woods where the trees are said to be the petrified bodies of ancient druids. At night, they move and whisper.

#### Description
- The trees have human-like shapes
- The leaves are always grey, regardless of season
- Strange lights appear at night
- The air smells of old magic and decay

#### Effects
- Those who enter the grove at night hear whispers
- The whispers reveal secrets but also tempt with dark knowledge
- Staying too long causes the visitor to become part of the grove

#### GM Secrets

1. **The Trees**: Are indeed petrified druids, trapped between life and death.

2. **The Whispers**: Are the druids' attempts to communicate.

3. **The Dark Knowledge**: Is actually the Old Night speaking through the druids.

#### Quest Hooks

1. **The Lost Traveler**: Someone entered the grove and never came out.
2. **The Druid's Secret**: One of the druids knows a ritual that could help the players.
3. **The Purification**: Players must find a way to free the druids' souls.

### 3. The Void Rift

#### Overview
A tear in reality where the Old Night bleeds through. It is slowly expanding, and nothing can stop it.

#### Description
- A swirling vortex of darkness
- The area around it is devoid of life
- Strange creatures emerge from it
- The air is cold and smells of nothing

#### Effects
- Those who get too close are pulled in
- The rift corrupts everything around it
- It is a source of Void-touched creatures

#### GM Secrets

1. **The Rift**: Is a wound in the fabric of reality caused by the Dark Plague.

2. **The Expansion**: Is accelerating as the Long Winter worsens.

3. **The Creatures**: Are fragments of the Old Night given form.

#### Quest Hooks

1. **The Sealing Ritual**: Players must find a way to close the rift.
2. **The Void-Touched**: A creature from the rift wants to help close it.
3. **The Sacrifice**: Closing the rift requires a terrible sacrifice.

## Writing Guidelines

### Tone and Style
- Maintain the dark fantasy tone established in the introduction
- Use evocative, atmospheric language
- Balance horror with wonder
- Keep descriptions grounded in the harsh reality of Eda
- Avoid modern anachronisms

### Consistency
- Use consistent terminology with Core Rulebook.md
- Reference established lore from other sections
- Maintain the "show, don't tell" principle
- Keep power levels consistent with game mechanics

### Structure
- Each location should have:
  - Clear heading

  - Key NPCs with personalities and secrets
  - Events and conflicts
  - GM-only secrets
