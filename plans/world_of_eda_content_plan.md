## Writing Guidelines

### Tone and Style
- Maintain the dark fantasy tone established in the introduction
- Use evocative, atmospheric language
- Balance horror with wonder
- Keep descriptions grounded in the harsh reality of Eda
- Avoid modern anachronisms (use "harpsichord" not "piano", etc.)

### Consistency
- Use consistent terminology with [`Core Rulebook.md`](../Core%20Rulebook.md)
- Reference established lore from other sections
- Maintain the "show, don't tell" principle
- Keep power levels consistent with the game mechanics

### Structure
- Each section should have:
  - Clear heading
  - Engaging opening
  - Detailed content
  - Practical GM advice (where applicable)
  - Plot hooks for adventures

### Format
- Use proper Markdown formatting
- Include appropriate image references (update [`plans/art_placement_plan.md`](art_placement_plan.md) as needed)
- Use blockquotes for flavor text
- Use tables for organized information

## Review Process

After each section is complete:
1. **Self-Review:** Read through for flow and consistency
2. **Cross-Reference:** Check against [`Core Rulebook.md`](../Core%20Rulebook.md) and [`Creature_Compendium.md`](../Creature_Compendium.md)
3. **GM Playtest:** Consider how a GM would use this information
4. **Adventure Hooks:** Ensure there are plot hooks for player adventures

After the entire document is complete:
1. **Full Read-Through:** Read entire World of Eda for consistency
2. **Peer Review:** Have another person review for clarity and errors
3. **Index Creation:** Create an index or table of contents
4. **Final Polish:** Check formatting, images, and cross-references
