## Introduction

Eda is a world caught in a celestial tug-of-war. While the common folk—struggling against the encroaching frost—might believe the world is flat, ancient scholars and the First Enlightened of the Age of Wonder understood the truth: Eda is a sphere suspended in a gravitational lock between two stars. The planet has existed for approximately 2.5 billion years, formed from the cooling remnants of a cosmic collision. Life, and specifically humanity, is said to have "grown" from the decaying remains of the Titans—the first sentient beings whose corpses now form the bedrock of the continents.

The name **Eda**, meaning "The Wondrous" in the High Tongue, dates back to the Age of Wonder (25,000 to 20,000 years ago). This was an era of unprecedented peace and technological advancement, powered by a deep understanding of the planet's unique placement. This era ended abruptly 20,000 years ago with the **Dark Plague**, a mass extinction event that wiped out 95% of the population within a single year. Today, in the year 423 after the coronation of **Korint, King of Kings**, the world is a shadow of its former self, with most knowledge of the past buried under ice or lost to the "etheric rot" that accompanies the plague.

## The Cosmology of the Twin Suns
The planet of Eda is caught in a stable Lagrange point between two suns, **Aurelius** and **Cinder**. This configuration creates a permanent radiation belt—the **Ether**—which acts as a persistent memory layer, capturing all thoughts and spirits outside of time. This radiation is the source of all magic, but as the suns age and the gravitational balance shifts, the Ether becomes unstable, allowing the "Old Night" (the vacuum of the void) to seep through.

## The Gods and the Urgudar
On Eda, "gods" are not ethereal spirits but the literal corpses of the **Titans** and **Urgudar** (Primal Giants). These beings roamed the world during its molten phase. Their bones are the mountains, their blood the iron in the earth, and their dying thoughts are what fuel the Ether. Religions on Eda are rarely about worshiping living deities; instead, they focus on appeasing the echoes of these dead giants or protecting the soul from the corruption of the Old Night.

## The Twin Suns of Eda

### The Celestial Dance: Two Suns, One World

Eda occupies one of the most extraordinary positions in the known universe—a gravitationally stable point between two stellar bodies positioned on opposite sides of the planet. Unlike typical binary star systems where planets orbit one or both stars, Eda is effectively "caught" in a delicate balance between two suns.

**The Two Suns:**

**Aurelius (The Greater Sun)**: A yellow dwarf star, approximately 1.2 times the mass of a typical main-sequence star, positioned at a distance of roughly 0.8 astronomical units from Eda. It provides the primary illumination and warmth to the planet's "bright day" hemisphere, casting a golden light that has inspired countless myths and legends.

**Cinder (The Lesser Sun)**: A red dwarf star, approximately 0.4 times the mass of a typical star, positioned at a distance of roughly 0.4 astronomical units from Eda on the opposite side. While smaller and dimmer, its proximity means it provides significant illumination to what would traditionally be the "night" hemisphere, bathing the world in a reddish-orange glow.

This configuration is maintained through a delicate gravitational equilibrium. Both suns orbit a common center of mass, with Eda trapped in a stable point between them. This arrangement is not permanent—astronomical calculations suggest it has existed for approximately 2.5 billion years and is gradually destabilizing as the stars age and lose mass.

### The Eternal Twilight: No True Night

On Earth, the day/night cycle is defined by the planet's rotation relative to a single sun. On Eda, this cycle is fundamentally different—there is no traditional "night" period. Because the two suns are positioned on opposite sides of the planet, one hemisphere always faces a sun. The concept of "darkness" is relative—there is only "brighter" and "dimmer" illumination.

**The Two-Phased Cycle:**

**Phase I (Aurelius-Dominant)**: When Aurelius is directly overhead, illumination is at maximum. This is the "bright day" period, lasting approximately 12 hours. The sky is a brilliant blue, shadows are sharp and short, and the golden light nourishes the land. This is when most agricultural work and outdoor activities occur.

**Phase II (Cinder-Dominant)**: When Cinder is directly overhead, illumination is dimmer, with a reddish-orange hue. This is the "dim day" period, also lasting approximately 12 hours. The sky takes on a sunset-like quality, shadows are long and soft, and the world is bathed in a gentle, melancholic light. This is often considered a time of rest, contemplation, and magical practice.

**The Twilight Zones:**

There are two brief periods (approximately 1-2 hours each) when neither sun is directly overhead. During these times, both suns are visible on opposite horizons, creating a spectacular "double-sunset" or "double-sunrise" phenomenon. The sky takes on a unique purple-violet hue as the light from both suns mixes in the atmosphere—a phenomenon known as the "Purple Sky" or "the Breath of the Gods."

These twilight zones are considered sacred times for rituals, magic, and meditation. The veil between worlds is thinnest during these moments, making them ideal for communing with the ether and the spirits that dwell within it.

**The Moons of Eda: Eclipses and True Darkness:**

While Eda has no traditional night, it is orbited by three moons that periodically create periods of true darkness through eclipses. These moons—known as the Pale Sister, the Crimson Brother, and the Void Child—have different orbital periods and sizes, creating a complex pattern of darkness that is both predictable and awe-inspiring.

**The Three Moons:**

**The Pale Sister (Lunara)**: The largest and brightest of Eda's moons, approximately 1.5 times the size of Earth's moon. It has a smooth, pale grey surface that reflects light beautifully. Its orbital period is approximately 28 days, and it creates the most common eclipses. When it passes between Eda and one of the suns, it creates a period of darkness lasting 1-2 hours.

**The Crimson Brother (Ignis)**: A smaller, reddish moon with a rugged, volcanic surface. Its orbital period is approximately 42 days, and it creates less frequent but more dramatic eclipses. Due to its reddish color, eclipses caused by Ignis bathe the world in a eerie red glow rather than true darkness.

**The Void Child (Umbra)**: The smallest and most mysterious of the three moons, barely visible to the naked eye. It has a dark, almost black surface that absorbs light rather than reflecting it. Its orbital period is approximately 56 days, and it creates the rarest eclipses. When Umbra passes in front of a sun, it creates the deepest darkness, as if the sun has been extinguished entirely.

**Single Sun Eclipses:**

The most common form of darkness on Eda occurs when one of the moons blocks one of the suns. These single sun eclipses happen approximately 3-4 times per month and create periods of partial darkness lasting 1-2 hours.

During a single sun eclipse:
- The blocked sun disappears from the sky, leaving only the other sun to provide illumination
- The world experiences a period of "partial night"—not true darkness, but significantly dimmer than the normal two-phase cycle
- Shadows become extremely long and distorted
- The temperature drops noticeably, sometimes by 10-15 degrees Fahrenheit
- Animals often become confused or agitated, their circadian rhythms disrupted
- The Ether becomes more turbulent, making magic more powerful but also more dangerous

Single sun eclipses caused by the Pale Sister create a gentle, peaceful darkness. Those caused by the Crimson Brother bathe the world in an eerie red glow and are often associated with omens or portents. Those caused by the Void Child create a profound, unsettling darkness that feels almost supernatural.

**The Double Eclipse: The Night of Two Suns:**

The rarest and most dramatic celestial event on Eda is the double eclipse—when both suns are blocked simultaneously by different moons. This occurs approximately once every 3-4 years, when the orbital paths of the moons align perfectly with both suns.

During a double eclipse:
- Both suns disappear from the sky simultaneously
- True darkness falls upon Eda for the first and only time in the cycle
- The darkness lasts approximately 2-3 hours, the longest period of darkness on the planet
- The temperature drops dramatically, sometimes by 20-30 degrees Fahrenheit
- The stars become visible for the first time, creating a spectacular display that most Edans have never seen
- The Ether reaches its lowest point, making magic nearly impossible
- The veil between worlds becomes thinnest, allowing spirits and entities from the Old Night to manifest more easily

The double eclipse is both a time of wonder and terror. Cultures across Eda have developed elaborate rituals and precautions for these events. Some celebrate the rare beauty of true darkness and the visible stars. Others fear it, believing it to be a moment when the Old Night's influence is strongest.

**Cultural and Religious Significance:**

The moons and their eclipses have profound cultural and religious significance across Eda:

**The Order of the Veiled Breath**: Views eclipses as times of extreme danger, when the "breath of the void" is strongest. During eclipses, especially double eclipses, members retreat to sealed chambers, perform additional purification rituals, and wear multiple layers of protective masks. They believe the Dark Plague first manifested during a double eclipse.

**The Iron Covenant**: Sees eclipses as opportunities for military operations. The darkness provides cover for surprise attacks, and the weakened Ether makes it harder for enemy spellcasters to defend themselves. The Covenant maintains detailed astronomical records to predict eclipses and plan campaigns around them.

**The Ashen Seers**: Consider eclipses sacred times for communion with the dying gods in the ether. They believe the darkness allows them to hear the whispers of the Urgudar more clearly. During double eclipses, the Ashen Seers perform their most dangerous rituals, seeking knowledge from the Old Night itself.

**The Church of the Sun-Bringer**: Views eclipses with mixed emotions—fear for the dying suns, but hope that they will return. They maintain "Ever-Fires" during eclipses to provide light and warmth, and use the darkness as a time for prayer and reflection on the fragility of their world.

**The Quiet Ones (Void-Worshippers)**: Celebrate eclipses, especially double eclipses, as moments when the Old Night's power is greatest. They perform dark rituals during these times, seeking to hasten the end of the world and merge with the void.

**Biological Effects of Eclipses:**

The periodic darkness caused by eclipses has shaped the evolution of life on Eda:

**Plant Life**: Many plants have evolved to respond to the sudden darkness of eclipses. Some flowers close their petals during eclipses, while others bloom specifically during these periods. Certain fungi release spores during eclipses, taking advantage of the reduced UV radiation.

**Animal Life**: Most animals have evolved instinctive responses to eclipses. Diurnal creatures seek shelter or become inactive during the darkness. Nocturnal creatures may emerge early, confused by the premature darkness. Some species have evolved to mate or give birth during eclipses, using the darkness as protection from predators.

**Humans**: Humans have developed cultural adaptations to eclipses. Most cultures have eclipse rituals, traditions, and precautions. Some people experience "eclipse sickness"—a temporary disorientation or nausea caused by the sudden change in light and the turbulent Ether. Those sensitive to magic often report visions or heightened sensitivity during eclipses, especially double eclipses.

**Astronomical Predictions:**

Because the orbital periods of the three moons are well understood, astronomers and astrologers can predict eclipses with remarkable accuracy. The First Enlightened of the Age of Wonder developed sophisticated mathematical models to track the moons' movements, and this knowledge has been preserved (though imperfectly) through the ages.

Today, several organizations maintain astronomical observatories:

**The Star-Towers of Aethelgard**: Ancient observatories with precise instruments that can track the moons' movements. The Glass-Singers use these predictions to time their most important rituals and experiments.

**The Iron Covenant's Astrologers**: Military astronomers who track eclipses for strategic purposes. Their predictions are used to plan campaigns and surprise attacks.

**The Ashen Seers' Sky-Watchers**: Mystics who observe the moons from high mountain peaks. Their predictions are less precise but are combined with spiritual interpretations.

**The Church of the Sun-Bringer's Calendar-Keepers**: Priests who maintain religious calendars based on eclipse cycles. They use these predictions to schedule festivals and holy days.

**The Impact on the Long Winter:**

As the Long Winter intensifies and the suns dim, the impact of eclipses has become more severe:

- The darkness during eclipses has become deeper and more profound
- The temperature drops during eclipses have become more extreme
- The Ether's turbulence during eclipses has become more unpredictable
- The veil between worlds during double eclipses has become thinner, allowing more entities from the Old Night to manifest
- The biological disruption caused by eclipses has become more pronounced, with more animals and humans experiencing "eclipse sickness"

Some scholars believe that as the suns continue to dim, eclipses will eventually become periods of permanent darkness. Others fear that the double eclipse will one day not end—that the moons will align and refuse to move, trapping Eda in eternal darkness.

For now, however, the moons continue their eternal dance, bringing periodic darkness to a world that has forgotten what true night looks like. Each eclipse is a reminder of the fragility of Eda's position between the suns, and each double eclipse is a glimpse into the darkness that awaits when the Long Winter finally claims the world.

### Climate and Weather Patterns

The constant dual illumination has profound effects on Eda's climate, creating a world of perpetual energy and unique weather phenomena.

**Temperature Regulation:**

Without a true night, there is no significant cooling period. Temperature variations are driven more by latitude and atmospheric circulation than by day/night cycles. The equatorial regions maintain a relatively constant temperature year-round, while polar regions experience seasonal variations due to axial tilt but never become truly dark.

This constant warmth has allowed life to flourish in regions that would be uninhabitable on Earth. The polar regions, while cold, support hardy ecosystems of specially adapted flora and fauna that have evolved to thrive in the dimmer light of Cinder.

**Atmospheric Circulation:**

The constant heating from both sides creates a unique atmospheric circulation pattern. There are two primary convection cells on each hemisphere, driven by the two suns. This results in more stable weather patterns than Earth, but also more extreme localized phenomena when the two systems interact.

Storms on Eda can be particularly intense, as the constant energy input from both suns fuels powerful weather systems. However, these storms are often predictable, following patterns that have been observed for millennia.

**The Radiation Belt (The Ether):**

The gravitational interaction between the two suns and Eda creates a permanent radiation belt around the planet—the Ether. This belt is not like Earth's Van Allen belts; it is a torus of high-energy particles trapped in the planet's magnetosphere.

The radiation interacts with the atmosphere to create permanent auroras visible from most of the planet's surface. These auroras are most spectacular during the Cinder-dominant phase, when the darker sky allows their colors to shine more brightly. The belt is strongest at the equator and weakens toward the poles, creating a gradient of magical potency across the planet.

**Precipitation Patterns:**

The constant heating means more evaporation and more precipitation overall. Rain is frequent but often brief and intense, falling in sudden downpours that nourish the land before the suns emerge again. There are no "dry seasons" in the traditional sense, only periods of more or less frequent rain.

The poles receive less precipitation due to colder air holding less moisture, creating vast ice sheets that reflect the dimmer light of Cinder and contribute to the planet's overall albedo.

### Biological Adaptations

Life on Eda has evolved remarkable adaptations to the constant dual illumination, creating ecosystems unlike any other in the known universe.

**Plant Life: The Spectrum of Survival**

Because Eda is never in total darkness (except during rare eclipses), plants have abandoned the "night cycle." Photosynthesis is a 24-hour process, leading to hyper-accelerated growth and massive botanical structures.
- **The Chromatic Shift**: Surface flora often exhibits dual-tone foliage. Leaves are frequently **dark emerald** or **black** on one side to absorb the low-frequency red light of Cinder, and **golden** or **pale green** on the other to reflect or process the intense radiation of Aurelius.
- **Sun-Trackers**: Species like the **Helio-Vines** have hydraulic stems that rotate 180 degrees every twelve hours, keeping their primary broadleaves aimed at whichever sun is currently dominant.
- **Bioluminescent Pollination**: Since there is no "night" to hide in, many flowers use bioluminescence not for light, but as a chemical signal that is only visible in the "Dim Day" (Cinder-dominant) phase, attracting specialized insects that sleep during the "Bright Day."

**Animal Life: The Eyes of Eda**

Evolution on Eda has focused heavily on optical and thermal regulation.
- **Dual-Iris Systems**: Many apex predators, such as the **Fjord-Cats**, possess dual irises or nictitating membranes that filter out the harsh UV of Aurelius while allowing them to see in the infrared spectrum of Cinder.
- **The Rest Gap**: Instead of one long sleep, most animals practice "polyphasic resting"—taking short, 2-hour naps during the transition periods (the Purple Sky twilight) when the shifting light creates long, confusing shadows that make hunting difficult.
- **Ether-Sensitivity**: Some fauna, like the **White-Stags**, have developed antlers or horns that act as lightning rods for the Ether. They can "feel" the approach of a solar flare or an eclipse hours before it happens, allowing them to seek shelter from the resulting magical turbulence.

**Humanity: A Radiation-Bred Heritage**

The people of Eda are biologically distinct from those of a single-sun world.
- **Ocular Evolution**: Edan eyes are larger, with a tapetum lucidum (reflective layer) that is more common in humans here than on Earth, giving their eyes a slight "sheen" in the red light of Cinder.
- **The Pineal Shield**: The human brain has evolved a thickened pineal gland that acts as a buffer against the Ether's radiation. Those with a "thin" buffer are often the ones who become **Witches** or **Seers**, as they can hear the whispers of the Ether more clearly, but at the cost of mental stability.
- **Metabolic Phasing**: Edans do not have a 24-hour circadian rhythm. Their bodies are tuned to a 12/12 cycle. They eat smaller meals twice as often and have a culture of "Mid-Day Rest" during the peak of Aurelius's heat, and "Mid-Dim Rest" during the coldest part of Cinder's dominance.

**The Underdark: Subterranean Ecosystems**

As the twin suns have weakened over the past millennia and the Long Winter has intensified, many species have retreated underground, creating complex subterranean ecosystems that exist entirely independent of the surface world. These underground realms, known collectively as the Underdark, have evolved unique adaptations to life in perpetual darkness.

**Why Creatures Went Underground:**

The retreat underground was a biological necessity as the surface environment destabilized.
- **Radiation Flux**: As the Etheric Belt weakened, "Radiation Storms" (high-energy particle bursts) became frequent. The Underdark provides a natural lead-and-magnetite shield of miles of rock.
- **Geothermal Sanctuary**: The surface temperature is tied to the dying stars, but the planet's core remains molten. Subterranean life relies on thermal energy rather than solar radiation.
- **Etheric Grounding**: The high concentration of iron and magnetite in the planet’s crust acts as a "Ground" for the Ether. Creatures sensitive to the constant mental "noise" of the radiation belt found silence in the deep.

**Subterranean Plant Life:**

While photosynthesis is impossible underground, the Underdark has evolved its own forms of plant life that thrive on alternative energy sources:

- **Chemosynthetic Fungi**: Massive fungal forests that derive energy from chemical reactions, particularly the breakdown of minerals and organic matter. These fungi can grow to enormous sizes, with some species forming "trees" that reach heights of over 30 feet.

- **Luminescent Mosses**: Mosses that have evolved to produce their own light through bioluminescence. These mosses carpet cavern walls and ceilings, creating ethereal, glowing landscapes in shades of blue, green, and violet. They feed on the trace minerals that seep through the rock.

- **Crystal Plants**: Strange, crystalline organisms that absorb and refract the faint magical energy that penetrates deep underground. These "plants" are actually silicon-based lifeforms that grow slowly over centuries, forming beautiful but dangerous crystal formations.

- **Thermal Blooms**: Flowers that grow near geothermal vents, absorbing heat energy directly. These blooms are often brightly colored and produce intense heat, creating small pockets of warmth in the cold underground.

**Subterranean Animal Life:**

The creatures of the Underdark have evolved remarkable adaptations to life in perpetual darkness:

- **Blind Hunters**: Predators that have lost their eyes entirely, relying instead on enhanced hearing, smell, and a primitive form of echolocation. These creatures are often pale, with translucent skin that shows their internal organs.

- **Burrowing Behemoths**: Massive, worm-like creatures that tunnel through the rock, consuming minerals and organic matter. Some species grow to lengths of over 100 feet, their bodies covered in thick, armored scales that protect them from the crushing pressure of deep earth.

- **Cave Swimmers**: Aquatic creatures that inhabit underground lakes and rivers. These species have evolved to navigate in complete darkness, using lateral line systems to detect movement in the water. Some have developed bioluminescent lures to attract prey.

- **Thermal Feeders**: Creatures that cluster around geothermal vents, feeding on the heat energy directly. These species often have specialized organs that can convert thermal energy into chemical energy, allowing them to survive without traditional food sources.

- **Scavenger Colonies**: Social insects and arthropods that form massive colonies, processing organic matter that falls from the surface or is brought in by water. These colonies can number in the millions, creating complex hive structures.

**The Food Chain:**

The underground ecosystem has developed its own food chain, independent of the surface world:

1. **Primary Producers**: Chemosynthetic fungi, luminescent mosses, and crystal plants form the base of the food chain, converting chemical energy, minerals, and trace magical energy into organic matter.

2. **Primary Consumers**: Small herbivores and detritivores feed on the fungi and mosses. These include cave-dwelling rodents, insect larvae, and small crustaceans.

3. **Secondary Consumers**: Predators hunt the primary consumers. These include blind hunters, cave spiders, and aquatic predators.

4. **Apex Predators**: The largest and most dangerous creatures sit at the top of the food chain. These include burrowing behemoths, massive cave-dwelling reptiles, and creatures that have been corrupted by the Old Night's influence seeping up from the planet's core.

**Human Interaction with the Underdark:**

Humans have had limited interaction with the Underdark, but what contact exists has been fraught with danger:

- **Mining Operations**: Dwarves and human miners have dug deep into the earth in search of precious metals and minerals. These operations often disturb the delicate balance of underground ecosystems, leading to conflicts with subterranean creatures.

- **Lost Expeditions**: Historical records mention numerous expeditions seeking "thought-glass" artifacts in the deep caverns. Most of these groups vanished, likely falling victim to the environmental hazards or the psychological toll of total darkness.

- **Refuge Communities**: Human populations fleeing the "Purges" of the Iron Covenant have established "Low-Light Settlements" in the upper strata. These cultures have begun to develop biological adaptations, such as heightened hearing and translucent skin.

- **Entropic Contamination**: In the deepest reaches, the boundary between the planet's core and the "Old Night" is failing. This creates zones of absolute zero temperature where matter loses its physical form.

**Environmental Hazards of the Deep:**

- **Absolute Darkness**: Unlike the surface, which is always bathed in either golden or red light, the deep earth is a void. Most native creatures hunt via echolocation or thermal sensing, giving them a massive biological advantage over surface-dwellers.
- **Tidal Shifts**: The gravitational pull of Aurelius and Cinder causes "Rock-Tides"—micro-movements in the tectonic plates that can collapse tunnels without warning.
- **Atmospheric Thinning**: Oxygen levels fluctuate based on the proximity to geothermal vents and the respiration of massive fungal forests.

**Notable Underdark Locations:**

Several notable Underdark locations are known to surface dwellers:

- **The Hollowed Warrens**: A vast network of tunnels beneath the Fjordlands, home to the Hollowed and their queen. These tunnels are said to connect to the sewers of Oakhaven, allowing the infected to move unseen beneath the city.

- **The Crystal Caverns of Aethelgard**: Massive caverns filled with bioluminescent crystal formations. The Glass-Singers of Aethelgard believe these caverns hold ancient knowledge from the Age of Wonder.

- **The Thermal Vents of the Deep North**: Geothermal vents that provide warmth in the frozen north. The Ashen Seers make pilgrimages to these vents, believing the heat is sacred.

- **The Buried City**: A legendary city from the Age of Wonder, said to have been swallowed by the earth during the Great Shattering. Some believe it still exists deep underground, preserved by ancient magic.

The Underdark represents one of the greatest mysteries and dangers of Eda. As the Long Winter intensifies and more creatures retreat underground, these subterranean ecosystems will become increasingly important to the survival of life on Eda—for better or for worse.

### The Science of the Ether: A Field Theory of Magic

Magic on Eda is not a mystical force, but a measurable interaction with the **Etheric Radiation Belt**. This belt is a torus of high-energy particles trapped within Eda’s magnetosphere, energized by the constant solar wind from Aurelius and Cinder.

**Etheric Resonance and Memory**
The Ether has a unique physical property: **sub-atomic resonance**. It acts like a global hard drive, recording the electrical impulses of intense thought and emotion. When a person dies, their "electric ghost" (their consciousness) is often caught in this belt.
- **The Persistence of Thought**: This is why "spirits" exist. They are not souls in a religious sense, but recorded patterns of data trapped in the atmosphere.
- **The Gradient of Power**: Because the belt is thicker at the equator, spells there have higher "amplitude" but lower "stability." In the polar regions, the thinness of the belt makes magic predictable but significantly less potent.

**Solar Alchemy: The Two Radiations**
Magic is categorized by which star’s radiation it "filters" through the Ether:
1. **Aurelius-Dominant (The Golden Weave)**: Short-wave, high-frequency radiation. It stimulates cellular growth, excites thermal energy (fire), and creates hard-light structures. It is the magic of **Creation** and **Vigor**.
2. **Cinder-Dominant (The Crimson Weave)**: Long-wave, low-frequency radiation. It breaks down molecular bonds, slows thermal vibration (frost/cold), and interferes with the nervous system (necromancy/decay). It is the magic of **Entrophy** and **Transformation**.
3. **The Purple Frequency**: The rarest form of magic, practiced only during the twilight transitions. It involves a 1:1 ratio of solar waves, allowing for the manipulation of space and time—the "folding" of the Ether itself.

**The Entropic Collapse (The Long Winter)**
The suns are cooling. As their solar wind weakens, the Etheric Belt is losing its "charge." This is the scientific cause of the **Long Winter**.
- **Thermal Leakage**: The Etheric Belt acts as a thermal blanket. As it thins, heat from the planet’s core and atmosphere leaks out into the void of the Old Night.
- **Void Contamination**: Without the pressure of the radiation belt, the "Old Night" (a literal vacuum energy from outside the solar system) is seeping into the atmosphere. This "Void-stuff" behaves like an anti-catalyst, erasing matter and suppressing life-functions.

### Cultural and Religious Science

The "Twin Sun" reality is not just a myth; it is the foundation of Edan sociology.

**The Theology of Equilibrium**
Most religions on Eda are based on the **Science of Balance**.
- **The Golden and the Red**: Aurelius is seen as the "Life-Giver" (Creative Radiation), while Cinder is the "Purifier" (Destructive/Transformative Radiation).
- **The Sin of Extremes**: Evil is rarely defined as "darkness" (since true darkness is rare), but as "stasis" or "imbalance." A world with only Aurelius would burn in eternal growth (cancerous and wild); a world with only Cinder would wither in eternal decay.
- **The Purple Rite**: The twilight periods are the only times when the two radiations mix perfectly. This is when marriages are performed, contracts are signed, and the dead are buried. It represents the "Point of Neutrality" where the Ether is most calm.

**Architecture of the Suns**
- **Dual-Faced Cities**: Settlements like Oakhaven are built with "Sun-Walls." During the Bright Day, heavy shutters are closed on the Aurelius-side to prevent overheating, while the Cinder-side remains open for ventilation. This flips during the Dim Day.
- **Light-Wells**: Since the suns are always on opposite horizons, buildings often feature central mirror-shafts (technology salvaged from the Age of Wonder) that pipe light into subterranean levels, ensuring even the poorest cellar-dwellers have "Sun-Time."
- **The Iron Cities**: The Iron Covenant builds with magnetite-infused stone, which naturally dampens the Ether's radiation. Their cities are "quiet" zones where magic is harder to use, serving as a biological sanctuary for those overwhelmed by the constant "hum" of the radiation belt.

**The Social Cycle**
- **The Second Sleep**: The concept of a "work day" is split. Laborers work for 6 hours of the Bright Day, sleep for 4 hours during the transition, work for 6 hours of the Dim Day, and sleep for 8 hours during the other transition.
- **Shadow-Tracking**: Navigating by shadows is an art form. Since there are two suns, every object casts two shadows of different colors and lengths. Expert scouts can tell their exact location and time by the angle and "mix" of their golden and red shadows.

**Art and Literature:**

The purple sky of the twilight zones is a common motif in art, representing the balance between the two suns and the sacred nature of the Ether. Many stories and myths revolve around the relationship between the two suns, often personifying them as lovers, siblings, or eternal rivals.

The "double-sunset" is considered the most beautiful sight in the world, and many artists have attempted to capture its beauty. Poets write of the moment when both suns are visible on opposite horizons, painting the sky in shades of gold, crimson, and violet. Musicians compose songs that attempt to capture the feeling of the twilight zones—the sense of being between worlds, between light and dark, between life and death.

### The Long Winter: The Death of the Suns

The Long Winter is not merely a weather pattern—it is the consequence of the two suns dying. As both suns reach the end of their stellar lifecycles, the Ether is weakening, and the barrier against the Old Night is growing brittle.

**The Nature of the Suns' Death:**

Both suns are reaching the end of their stellar lifecycles, though at different rates. Aurelius is expanding into a red giant, consuming its fuel faster and growing larger but cooler. Cinder is cooling as it exhausts its hydrogen fuel, its light becoming dimmer and more red.

The gravitational balance that keeps Eda between them is destabilizing. As the suns lose mass, their gravitational pull weakens, and the delicate equilibrium that has maintained Eda's position for billions of years is beginning to break down. Eventually, Eda will either fall into one of the suns or be flung out into the void.

**The Consequences:**

As the suns dim, the Ether weakens. The radiation belt is becoming less stable, and the barrier that holds back the Old Night is growing brittle. The Long Winter is the Old Night breathing through the cracks, drawing heat out of the world and bringing supernatural cold.

Each winter is potentially the last. The Long Winter is not a natural weather pattern—it is a supernatural phenomenon, a manifestation of the Old Night's hunger reaching out to consume the warmth of life. The cold is not natural; it is the cold of non-existence, the cold of the Void.

**The Scientific Reality:**

The radiation belt is collapsing. The atmosphere is thinning. The planet is slowly losing its ability to retain heat. Without intervention, Eda will become a frozen wasteland within a few centuries, a lifeless rock drifting between two dying stars.

The weakening of the Ether is also affecting the planet's magnetic field, which is generated by the interaction between the radiation belt and the planet's core. As the magnetic field weakens, the planet becomes more vulnerable to cosmic radiation and solar flares, further accelerating the collapse of the ecosystem.

**The Hope:**

The suns are not dead yet. There is still time to reverse the Long Winter, to strengthen the Ether, and to restore the barrier against the Old Night. The Mythos tells of the **First Silence**, a moment when the light of the Golden Void flickered. From the shadows of that flicker, the **Old Night** was born—a hunger that sought to consume the light. To survive, the Titans had to manifest; they had to become "real." Eda was not "built"; it was the result of a great collision. Two massive Titans, whose names are forgotten, locked in an eternal embrace to shield a pocket of the Golden Void from the encroaching Old Night. Their bodies fused, their blood became the oceans, and their dying breaths became the atmosphere. This planet—Eda—is a living sarcophagus. The radiation belt that circles it is the lingering heat of that ancient collision, now known as the **Ether**.

#### The Imprisonment of the Old Night
The Old Night was not defeated, merely locked away. The core of Eda is said to be a "Cracked Prison" who wear the strongest shards of the Night are kept in check by the planet's internal heat. As the twin suns dim and the world cools, the prison grows brittle. The **Long Winter** is not just a weather pattern; it is the Old Night slowly breathing through the cracks in the world.

### Chapter 4: The Divine & The Faiths

#### The Silent Gods: Fragments of Memory
The gods of Eda are not celestial beings in the traditional sense. They are the **Urgudar**—the first giants and titans who walked the earth when it was still molten. Their bodies form the bedrock of the continents, and their dying thoughts permeate the ether.

#### The Order of the Veiled Breath (The Masked ones)
The most prominent religion in the Fjordlands, the **Order of the Veiled Breath**, was born from the trauma of the **Dark Plague**. They believe that the plague was a physical manifestation of "impurity" leaked from the **Old Night**.

*   **The Mask**: Members are never seen in public without a mask. These masks are often carved from the bone of ancestors or forged from cold iron. They serve as a filter, both physical and spiritual, to keep the "breath of the void" at bay. To show one's face to a stranger is considered a grave sin and a biological risk.
*   **The Five Washings**: Devout followers wash their hands, face, and feet with a mixture of salt and lye five times a day: at dawn, mid-morning, noon, mid-afternoon, and dusk. These rituals are performed with surgical precision.
*   **The Pantheon of Purity**: They have mixed the names of ancient titans with the "Saints of the Plague."
    *   **Saint Valerius**: Often depicted with a bird-like mask, he is the patron of medicine and "the clean cut."
    *   **The Mother of Ash**: A titan whose volcanic body provides the lye used in their purifications.

#### The Iron Covenant: Tyranny in the Name of Order
A militant sect that follows the teachings of **Korint**. They believe that only through absolute iron rule can humanity survive the Long Winter. They view magic as a chaotic infection that must be contained or "cauterized." They are known for their massive iron citadels and their "Inquisitors" who hunt those touched by the void.

#### The Ashen Seers: Purification through Frost
Found primarily in the deep north, these mystics believe that the **Long Winter** is a cleansing fire of ice. They walk naked through the snow, their skin turned blue and leathery by the cold. They seek to "freeze" their thoughts to match the stillness of the dead gods in the ether.

#### The Church of the Sun-Bringer: A Flickering Flame
A dying faith that clings to the hope that the twin suns will one day return to their full glory. They maintain "Ever-Fires" in small villages, providing the only warmth against the encroaching dark. They are often targets of the Iron Covenant, who view their optimism as a dangerous delusion.

#### Secret Cults and Void-Worshippers (The Quiet Ones)
In the shadows of the great ruins, some have begun to worship the **Old Night** itself. They believe that survival is impossible and that the only mercy is to hasten the end. They speak in whispers and are said to be able to "un-make" objects and people by tapping into the lack of existence.

### Chapter 5: The History of the Wolf-Age

#### The Era of Prosperity (The Golden Age)
The **Age of Wonder**, occurring between 25,000 and 20,000 years ago, represents the zenith of human civilization on Eda. During this time, the "giants" were not yet distant myths but active, though slumbering, presences in the landscape. The name **Eda**, meaning "Wondrous" in the High Tongue, was coined to describe a world who wear the ether was calm and magic was a tool for creation rather than a source of corruption.

Great spires of white stone and "thought-glass" rose above the forests, linked by bridges of pure light. There was no hunger, as the radiation from the twin suns was harnessed to feed the soil and the soul alike. This was the era of the **First Enlightened**, who understood the world was a sphere caught in a celestial dance.

#### The First Frost: The Warning Signs
The end of the Golden Age did not begin with a bang, but with a cough. A subtle shift in the radiation belt—the ether—caused the first "thinning." The giants, whose decaying bodies formed the mountains and valleys, began to stir in their eternal sleep. The warmth of the twin suns seemed to dim, and the first recorded frosts appeared in the tropical belts. 

#### The Dark Plague (The Great Extinction)
Roughly 20,000 years ago, the **Pustule Plague** (or the **Dark Plague**) struck. It was a biological catastrophe without precedent. Within a single year, 95% of the global population perished. 

The disease manifested as hard, black warts that formed in clusters around the joints and throat. These warts would eventually engorge with a toxic, grey puss and explode, spreading the infection to anyone nearby. It was not merely a physical ailment; it was an "etheric rot" that erased the memories of the dying, ensuring that the knowledge of the Age of Wonder died with its victims.

#### The Age of Wolves: The Collapse of Empires
In the vacuum left by the plague, the survivors descended into savagery. This 19,000-year gap is known as the **Silent Era** or the **Age of Wolves**. Humanity forgot how to read, how to forge "thought-glass," and how to live in peace. Small tribes fought over the rusted scraps of the giants' armor. The world became a place of scarcity and fear, who wear the only law was the blade and the wolf.

#### The Current Era: The Last Stand of Humanity
The modern calendar begins with **Korint, King of Kings**. 423 years ago, Korint united the fractured Fjordlands and the scattered southern tribes under a single, bloody banner. He was a man of immense ambition who sought to reclaim the title of "God" for humanity. He reset the calendar to mark his coronation, declaring the "Rebirth of Eda."

However, Korint's empire lasted only as long as he did. Upon his death, his sons tore the kingdom apart, leading to the current state of fragmented city-states like **Oakhaven** and the **Iron Hold**. Today, the **Long Winter** has truly begun; the suns grow colder, and the void between them grows wider. Humanity is no longer building; it is merely trying to avoid the final breath.

## Part II: The Occult & The Arcane

### Chapter 6: Etheric Engineering

Magic on Eda is often called "The Craft" or "The Song," but at its core, it is the manipulation of the Etheric Radiation Belt through biological conduits.

#### The Cost of Conductance: Biological Scars
Human nerves are not designed to carry the raw amplitude of the Ether. Every time a practitioner channels radiation, they suffer **Etheric Burn**.
- **Golden Scars**: Overuse of Aurelius-radiation leads to "calcification"—the skin turns to a translucent, glass-like substance.
- **Crimson Scars**: Overuse of Cinder-radiation leads to "atrophy"—the flesh becomes grey, cold, and eventually turns to ash.
- **The Void Stain**: Tapping into the Old Night results in "deletion"—parts of the user’s memory or physical body simply cease to exist, leaving smooth, featureless voids.

### Chapter 7: Biological Occultism

#### The Science of Vital Fluids
Liquids are the best conductors for Etheric energy. In Eda, "casting" usually involves the expenditure of body fluids to bridge the gap between the user’s mind and the atmosphere.
*   **Blood (The High Conductor)**: Hemoglobin, rich in iron (the blood of the Titans), is the most potent catalyst. Blood magic is essentially "short-circuiting" the Ether directly into the caster's veins. It is efficient but attracts the attention of "Echoes" (recorded consciousnesses) in the Ether.
*   **Excreta (Low-Grade Filters)**: Urine and spit are used as "grounding" agents. The **Masked Ones** use chemically treated saliva to saturate their lye-filters, creating a biological shield that neutralizes "Void-drift" in the air.

#### Bone-Runic Resonance
Human bone is a biological record of the Titan-heritage. Because the Titans were made of radiation-dense matter, their "descendants'" bones act as resonators for the Ether.
- **Ancestral Echoing**: Carving runes into bone creates a permanent "frequency" that matches the ancestor's recording in the Ether. It allows a user to "download" skills or memories from the past.
- **Bone Masks**: These are not just religious symbols; they are wearable antennas that filter out the "noise" of the modern, dying Ether and tune the wearer to the "clean" frequencies of the Age of Wonder.

#### Reproductive Surge
The act of procreation involves a massive biological energy release. In the language of Edan science, this is a **Local Etheric Spike**. It is the only time a human can naturally "overwrite" a section of the persistent memory layer. This is why "Love Rituals" or "Birth Oaths" are so magically binding; they are literally hard-coded into the planet's data layer.

### Chapter 9: The Fjordlands

The Fjordlands are the bruised knuckles of Eda—a series of jagged, ice-choked inlets and granite cliffs that were once the literal "ribs" of a fallen titan. It is a land of verticality and salt-spray, who wear the only thing more dangerous than the terrain is the weather.

#### Oakhaven: The Last Ember
![The Village of Oakhaven](images/village.png)

Oakhaven is the largest settlement in the Fjordlands, built within the hollowed-out skull of a minor giant. The "Oak" for which it is named is a massive, petrified tree that grows from the giant's eye-socket, its roots reaching deep into the ether-veins. It is one of the few places where the **Order of the Veiled Breath**, the **Iron Covenant**, and the **Church of the Sun-Bringer** exist in a fragile, tense alliance. The city's fires are maintained by the **Sun-Bringers**, who are tolerated only because they keep the frost-ghouls at bay.

With a population of approximately 3,000, Oakhaven is a beacon of civilization in a dying world—but a beacon that flickers with each passing winter. Refugees arrive daily, fleeing the encroaching cold and the horrors that come with it. Food stores dwindle, tempers flare, and the shadows in the sewers grow longer.

##### The Skull Gate (Main Entrance)
A massive stone archway carved from the giant's jawbone, the Skull Gate is the only way in or out of Oakhaven. It is guarded around the clock by Iron Covenant soldiers who wear blindfolds to "see the truth" and prevent the "unclean" from entering. Refugees line up for hours, hoping for entry, while the guards process them with brutal efficiency. Those deemed "tainted" by magic or the Void are turned away to face the winter alone.

##### The Market District
The heart of the city, where muddy streets are perpetually slick with ice and the air smells of smoke, salted fish, and desperation. This is where commerce happens, where deals are struck in shadows, and where the pulse of the city can be felt.

**The Muddy Tankard**: The central tavern and inn, owned by Beren, a massive one-armed man who lost his arm to a troll. Beren vaults the bar with a cudgel if fights break out, and his establishment is the safest place in the city—for those who can afford the ale. The floor space is rented out to travelers who have nowhere else to sleep.

**Orin's Smithy**: The only smithy in Oakhaven approved by the Iron Covenant. Orin, a muscular man with burn scars on his arms, forges weapons and armor—but secretly hates the Covenant. He hides a family heirloom in his workshop, an artifact that glows with an inner light and whispers secrets to him at night.

**Elara's Herbalist**: A small shop filled with drying herbs, strange potions, and the smell of old magic. Elara, an elderly woman with knowing eyes and fingers stained with herbs, was once an Ashen Seer who left the order. She sells remedies that work, but some require ingredients she won't discuss.

**Garris' Goods**: The general store, where prices are inflated five times normal due to scarcity. Garris, a thin man with nervous eyes, sells rope, torches, rations, and blankets—anything a traveler might need to survive the winter.

**The Black Market**: Hidden beneath the market square, accessible only through a secret passage in Grek "The Rat's" corner of the Muddy Tankard. Here, poisons, lockpicks, and stolen goods change hands. Grek, a tiny hunchbacked man who wears six layers of rags, knows everything that enters or leaves the city. He doesn't want money—he wants secrets and strange artifacts.

##### The High Terrace
Built on a hill overlooking the city, these stone houses belong to the merchants, the nobles, and those with power. Private guards patrol the streets, and the gates are locked at night.

**Mayor's Manor**: The seat of local government and the residence of Mayor Valen. Valen is pale, sweating, and wears velvet even in the bitter cold, leaning on a cane. He is cowardly but cunning, terrified of both Hrothgar of the Iron Covenant and his own people rioting. His secret: he is already infected by a slow strain of the Hollowed, and hides the black veins spreading under his collar.

**The Archive**: A library of pre-plague knowledge, restricted to those with special permission. The books here contain forgotten lore, maps of the world before the Dark Plague, and secrets that could change everything—if anyone could read them.

**Temple of the Veiled Breath**: The headquarters of the Masked Ones in Oakhaven. Members are never seen without their bone or iron masks, which serve as filters to keep the "breath of the void" at bay. They perform the Five Washings with surgical precision, and their presence is both comforting and unsettling to the citizens.

**Sun-Bringer's Chapel**: A small, flickering flame maintained by the dwindling faithful of the Church of the Sun-Bringer. They believe the twin suns will one day return to their full glory, and their eternal fire provides the only warmth against the encroaching dark. They are often targets of the Iron Covenant, who view their optimism as a dangerous delusion.

##### The Slums (The Mud-Pits)
A maze of tents and lean-tos built outside the main stone walls, where gangs and desperation rule. Security is minimal, and those who live here survive by their wits.

**Soup Kitchen**: Run by Mara, a young priestess of the Hearth. Her robes are perpetually stained with blood and mud as she attempts to heal the influx of wounded refugees. She is the primary advocate for the city's poor, often clashing with the Iron Covenant over resource allocation.

**Refugee Camp**: Overflowing with people fleeing the Long Winter, the camp is a place of suffering and hope. Diseases spread quickly here, and the Iron Covenant conducts periodic "purges" to remove those suspected of carrying the Hollowed infection.

##### The Docks
The frozen harbor of Oakhaven, where ships are trapped in the ice and used as housing. The ice fishing provides food, but the waters hold dangers beneath.

**Captain Jora's Smuggling Operation**: Jora, a scarred woman with one eye, runs a smuggling operation from her trapped ship. She deals in contraband, information, and passage for those who need to disappear. Fight clubs are held on the ice at night, where desperate men and women battle for coin and the chance to eat.

##### The Undercity (Sewers)
The ancient drainage system beneath Oakhaven, home to giant rats, slime creatures, and worse. The walls here breathe, and the air is thick with methane and the whispers of something ancient.

**The Hollowed Nest**: The secret lair of the Hollowed Queen, a powerful entity controlling the infected in the sewers. She was once a noblewoman who made a pact with the Old Night, and now she waits, patient and hungry, for the walls to crumble.

##### Notable Figures of Oakhaven

**Mayor Valen**: A pale, sweating man who clings to his authority despite his failing health. He is secretly infected by a slow-acting strain of the **Hollowed** virus, a fact he hides behind high collars and velvet robes.

**Captain Vorn**: The corrupt head of the City Watch. He is a superstitious bully who uses the Iron Covenant’s mandates to extort local merchants and refugees.

**Mara of the Hearth**: A young priestess who manages the city’s soup kitchens. Her hair has turned grey from the sheer stress of attempting to sustain Oakhaven’s 3,000 residents on dwindling supplies.

**Grek "The Rat"**: A hunchbacked information broker who operates from the Muddy Tankard. He deals exclusively in secrets and pre-plague artifacts, having no use for the inflated currency of the Fjordlands.

**Beren**: The one-armed proprietor of the Muddy Tankard. A veteran of the Troll-Wars, he maintains a strict neutrality within his tavern, which is often the only neutral ground for the city's warring factions.

**Orin the Smith**: A muscular man scarred by his forge, Orin secretly harbors a deep hatred for the Iron Covenant. He maintains a collection of "thought-glass" artifacts salvaged from his ancestors.

**Elara the Herbalist**: A former Ashen Seer who fled the harshness of the north. She provides medicine to the poor, though many suspect her tinctures involve forbidden blood-alchemy.

**Captain Jora**: A one-eyed smuggler who operates out of a ship trapped in Oakhaven's frozen harbor. She is one of the few individuals who still knows the treacherous sea-routes to the hidden city of Aethelgard.

- **The Refugee Influx**: The population of Oakhaven has tripled in a decade. The city's infrastructure, designed for a minor settlement, is collapsing under the weight of thousands of displaced families.
- **Etheric Instability**: The worsening Long Winter has made the Ether turbulent. The Iron Covenant has responded by conducting "Purification Checks," executing anyone showing signs of high radiation sensitivity.
- **The Hollowed Spread**: The city's sewers have become a breeding ground for the Hollowed—a post-plague mutation where the victim’s internal heat is entirely consumed by the Void.

##### Historical Truths of Oakhaven

- **The Living Skull**: The giant's skull upon which Oakhaven is built is not entirely dead. The bone still conducts Etheric signals, leading to frequent "collective dreaming" among those who live in the residential skull-cavities.
- **The Sun-Shard**: Hidden deep beneath the city's foundations is a piece of ancient "Solar-Silicon." This artifact was intended to act as a secondary heater for the city during the Age of Wonder, but its activation now would likely attract Void-entities like a beacon.
- **The Resistance**: A clandestine network of laborers and smiths, known as the "Glass-Forgers," exists within the city. They seek to restore the technological autonomy of the Fjordlands and overthrow the Iron Covenant's religious monopoly.

#### The High Barrow: The Cracked Prison
Located on the highest peak of the Fjordlands, the High Barrow is an ancient structure of "thought-glass" and iron. It is believed to be the primary seal for a shard of the **Old Night**. The air around the Barrow is so thin and cold that breath turns to ice instantly, and the ether screams with the whispers of the imprisoned.

#### The Iron Hold: The Mountain of Despair
The seat of the **Iron Covenant**. It is a fortress carved directly into a mountain of pure magnetite. The magnetic fields here are so strong they interfere with the ether, making magic nearly impossible to cast but providing a natural defense against the void-entities. The Covenant uses this "dead zone" to train their inquisitors.

#### Frostspire: The Ancient Watch
A mountain village built around an ancient watchtower that predates the Age of Wonder. The villagers are descendants of the original guardians, maintaining a vigil against threats from the north. With a population of approximately 400, Frostspire is small but resilient, its people hardened by generations of watching the horizon for enemies that never come—until now.

##### The Watchtower (Frostspire)
An ancient stone tower reaching into the clouds, its weathered stones bearing runes that glow faintly in the dark. The top floor houses the Ever-Watch lantern, a magical light that has burned for millennia, a beacon against the encroaching darkness. The lower levels contain archives of pre-plague observations—records of weather patterns, creature migrations, and phenomena that no longer exist. In the basement, accessible only through a hidden door, lies ancient technology of unknown purpose, humming with power that no one dares to touch.

##### The Village Square
The heart of Frostspire, where the central well contains water that never freezes, no matter how cold the winter becomes. The square is surrounded by stone buildings that have stood for centuries, their foundations anchored deep in the mountain rock.

**The Frosty Mug**: The local tavern and gathering place, owned by Jorin, a rotund, cheerful man missing several teeth. Jorin is a gossip who knows everyone's business, and his tavern is where news from Oakhaven and the outside world is shared. He secretly spies for the Iron Covenant, but only because they threatened his family.

**General Store**: A small shop stocked with basic supplies, hunting equipment, and furs. The prices are high, but the quality is unmatched—everything here is made to survive the harshest winter.

**The Watchman's Hall**: The meeting place for the village council, where elders debate decisions and plan for the future. The walls are lined with trophies from legendary beasts killed by Frostspire's hunters over the centuries.

##### The Hunter's Quarter
The homes of Frostspire's hunters and scouts, built close to the village walls for quick access to the wilderness. The air here smells of curing hides and woodsmoke.

**The Trophy Hall**: A long building displaying the remains of legendary beasts killed by Frostspire's hunters. The centerpiece is the skull of a dragon, its teeth still sharp after centuries. Hunters bring their trophies here to prove their worth and earn the respect of their peers.

**The Tannery**: Where animal hides are processed into leather and fur. The smell is overpowering, but the work is essential for survival in the north. The tanner, a silent woman named Brynja, knows secrets of hide preparation that have been passed down for generations.

##### The Healer's Cottage
A small cottage at the edge of the village, surrounded by a garden of medicinal herbs that only grow at this altitude. The healer, Mira, is a young woman with gentle hands and a knowing smile. She is compassionate but guarded about her methods, for she uses forbidden blood magic to heal the sick—magic that could get her executed if discovered.

In the basement of the cottage, hidden behind a false wall, Mira conducts experiments with remedies that push the boundaries of what is considered acceptable. She is currently caring for a patient with a mysterious illness that no one can identify—an illness that seems to be spreading.

##### The Old Mine
An abandoned iron mine at the base of the mountain, its entrance sealed by village elders decades ago. Rumors say the mine contains ancient weapons from the Age of Wonder, but those who have ventured inside have never returned. Recently, strange lights have been seen near the sealed entrance, and the ground occasionally shakes as if something is trying to break out.

##### Notable Figures of Frostspire

**Elder Thorne**: The stoic village leader who bears the responsibility of maintaining the Watchtower. He is one of the few alive who understands the tower's true function as a gravitational stabilizer.

**Mira the Healer**: A woman who uses a mix of herbalism and "crimson-vein" alchemy to treat the villagers. She secretly cares for several patients afflicted with an unknown, Ether-borne wasting disease.

**Kael the Hunter**: A former Iron Covenant soldier who deserted after witnessing the "cauterization" of a southern village. He provides the village with the majority of its meat and leather.

**Old Silas**: The blind keeper of the Ever-Watch lantern. He claims to hear the "hum" of the tower, which he interprets as the voice of the world itself.

**Brynja the Tanner**: A silent woman who has discovered a vein of "pre-plague iron" in the old mine, which she uses to reinforce the village's tools.

##### Regional Realities

- **The Northern Migration**: Strange, radiation-warped creatures are moving south from the deep polar regions, driven by the intensifying cold of the Long Winter.

##### Hidden History of Frostspire

- **The Shard-Well**: The tower was built not just to watch, but to contain. Deep in its foundations lies a "Void-Shard"—a piece of non-existence that was used as a power source during the Golden Age, now a ticking clock of entropy.
- **Aethelgardian Interest**: The "Stranger" in the village is an undercover **Glass-Singer** from Aethelgard, attempting to recalibrate the tower's beacon to protect their own city's domes.

#### Ashwater: The Village of Ash
A fishing village built on the shores of a lake with water that turns to ash when disturbed by the living. The villagers have adapted to this strange phenomenon and developed unique traditions around it. With a population of approximately 300, Ashwater is a place of mystery and quiet desperation, its people bound to the ash lake by forces they don't fully understand.

##### The Ash Lake
A mysterious lake with grey, ash-like water that ripples like normal water but turns to grey ash upon contact with living flesh. The fish that live here have strange properties—eating them grants visions but causes corruption over time. The lake is said to be connected to the Old Night, a tear in reality where non-existence bleeds through.

The ash that forms from the lake is collected and used by the villagers for various purposes—building, insulation, and in rituals conducted by the shrine keeper. The ash never decays, and objects made from it last forever, as if preserved in a moment of non-existence.

##### The Village Proper
Houses built on stilts to avoid the ash that occasionally rises from the lake during certain phases of the moon. The architecture is unique, with bridges connecting the buildings and nets strung between them to catch anything that might fall into the ash.

**The Ashen Net**: The local tavern and inn, built on the largest stilt platform. The innkeeper, Finn, is a strong, weathered man missing an ear to frostbite. He is practical and skeptical of outsiders, but his ale is the best in the village. The tavern is where villagers gather to share stories and warnings about the lake.

**The Drying Racks**: Where the strange fish from the lake are processed. The fish are gutted and hung to dry, their flesh turning a translucent grey. The dried fish is a staple food source, though eating too much causes strange dreams and eventually madness.

**The Shrine of Ashes**: A religious site where offerings are made to the lake. The shrine keeper, Brother Silas, is a thin, gaunt man who wears robes of ash-grey cloth and speaks in whispers. He performs rituals that the villagers believe keep the ash at bay, though in truth, his rituals are feeding the Old Night and widening the tear.

##### The Fishermen's Docks
Boats made of specially treated wood that can navigate the ash-water without turning to ash. The boats are painted with symbols that the fishermen claim protect them from the lake's influence, though no one knows the symbols' true meaning.

##### Notable Figures of Ashwater

**Elder Mara**: An ancient woman whose skin has taken on the grey, powdery texture of the lake itself. She is the only one who can navigate the village blindly, guided by the "resonance" of the ash.

**Finn the Fisherman**: A man whose livelihood depends on the strange, toxic fish of the lake. He is a pragmatist who views the lake not as a curse, but as a biological anomaly to be managed.

**Lira the Netmaker**: A young woman whose specialty is weaving nets from "cauterized silk"—a material that does not disintegrate when in contact with the ash-water.

**The Pit-Keepers**: A group of scarred elders led by **Old Haldor** who maintain the perimeter of the ash pits, ensuring that whatever "crawls out" is returned to the void.

#### Aethelgard: The City of Glass

![Map of Eda (Zoomed)](images/maps/EdaMapZoomed.png)

The legendary city of the Age of Wonder, now frozen in time beneath massive glass domes. Most believe it's a myth, but those who have seen it speak of impossible wonders and terrible dangers. The city was preserved at the height of the Golden Age, a perfect snapshot of a civilization that once knew peace and prosperity.

Inside, the city is perfectly preserved—buildings stand intact, streets are clean, and the air is breathable but strange, carrying the scent of ozone and memories. ##### The Glass Domes
Massive structures that rise like bubbles from the forest floor, their surfaces smooth and translucent. The domes are connected by tunnels of the same indestructible material, forming a network that once housed thousands. The glass is warm to the touch, humming with a faint energy that makes the skin tingle.

The domes are beginning to crack. Small fissures have appeared at the bases, and strange mists seep out at night. The Glass-Singers claim the cracks are growing, that the city is dying after millennia of preservation.

##### The Central Plaza
The heart of the ancient city, a vast open space paved with white stone that still gleams after twenty thousand years. The plaza is dominated by three structures: the Fountain of Memory, the Statue of the First, and the Library of Echoes.

**The Fountain of Memory**: A fountain of clear water that shows visions of the past to those who gaze into it. Drink from it, and you experience memories of the ancient inhabitants—their joys, their sorrows, their final moments as the plague came. The visions are addictive, and those who drink too often lose themselves in the past, becoming Echoes.

**The Statue of the First**: A massive monument to the first enlightened, the humans who first understood the nature of the ether and the twin suns. The statue is made of a metal that doesn't exist anymore, its surface etched with runes that glow faintly at night. Some say the statue is alive, that it watches those who pass and judges them.

**The Library of Echoes**: A vast building containing all knowledge from the Age of Wonder. The books are made of a material that doesn't decay, their pages filled with writing in languages that have been forgotten. The library is guarded by Echoes—ghostly fragments of the dead who still remember the knowledge they once possessed.

##### The Residential District
Preserved homes of the ancient inhabitants, their doors still standing open as if the owners just stepped out. Some homes still contain personal belongings—clothing, tools, children's toys frozen in mid-play. Walking through the district is like walking through a ghost town where the ghosts are still present.

The Echoes are strongest here. Ghostly figures can be seen and heard, fragments of the ancient inhabitants going about their daily routines. They don't notice visitors, caught in loops of memory that repeat endlessly. Some Echoes can be communicated with through the right rituals, but they are fragmented and often confused.

##### The Temple of Light
Where the ancient gods were worshipped, a massive structure of white stone and gold leaf. The temple contains artifacts of immense power—devices that can manipulate the ether, weapons that can level mountains, and knowledge that could change the world.

But the temple is also where the Dark Plague originated. An experiment gone wrong released the plague that wiped out 95% of humanity. The temple is now a place of danger, its lower levels corrupted by the plague's residue. Those who venture too deep never return, or return changed.

##### The Undercity
Beneath the main city lies the machinery that maintained the domes. Massive devices of unknown purpose hum with power, their surfaces etched with instructions in forgotten languages. The Undercity is home to the Glass-Singers, the last descendants of the ancient inhabitants who stayed behind to maintain the city.

The Glass-Singers are translucent, their skin almost transparent, their eyes reflecting light like mirrors. They speak in songs, their voices harmonizing in ways that human throats can't reproduce. They are dying, their bodies fading as the domes crack and the city's power fails.

##### Notable Elements of Aethelgard

**The Glass-Singers**: The last biological remnants of the city's original inhabitants. Their skin is translucent due to extreme "Golden-Weave" radiation exposure, and their speech has evolved into harmonic frequencies used to repair the "Thought-Glass" of the domes.

**The Echoes**: These are not ghosts, but high-resolution data-patterns stored in the city's Etheric field. They represent the collective memories of the millions who died during the Great Extinction.

**The Guardian**: A massive autonomous construct of solidified light and silicon. It follows logic-loops programmed 20,000 years ago, many of which are now corrupted by the thinning of the Ether.

##### Aethelgardian Reality

- **The Domes**: These are not glass, but **Force-Crystallized Ether**. They were designed to protect the city from the first "thinning" of the radiation belt, but they require a constant "charge" of human consciousness to remain stable.
- **Temporal Dilation**: The intense concentration of Etheric energy within the domes causes a local warping of spacetime. This is a scientific byproduct of the "Purple Frequency" magic used to build the city.
- **The Song of Preservation**: The Glass-Singers' rituals are actually a form of **Acoustic Engineering**, using sound waves to stabilize the molecular structure of the domes.

##### Hidden History of Aethelgard

- **The Origin of the Rot**: The Dark Plague was a biological weapon created to "cauterize" the Etheric connection of rival civilizations. It backfired when it mutated to consume the very "thought-data" it was meant to protect.
- **The Core Cracks**: The fissures in the domes are caused by the suns' gravitational drift. As Eda moves away from its central point, the domes' structural integrity is being torn apart by tidal forces.
